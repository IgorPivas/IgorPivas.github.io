Intro

I'm a Mechanical Systems Engineer who has had a passion for all things engineering for as long as I can remember. Ever since I could hold a screwdriver, I was taking apart any toy or broken tool I got my hands on and trying to figure out how they worked; later, I even learned how to put them back together or even fix them.

This interest led me to take every engineering-related course in school and follow any tutorial for building small robots that I could find. I even got to create some pretty cool robots along the way, such as a sumo bot and a firefighting robot which both scored second place in the year-end competitions. These experiences led me to become a Mechanical Systems Engineer by first going to Conestoga College for the Mechanical Engineering Technology program and then bridging into the Bachelor of Mechanical Systems Engineering to develop my skills further and dive deeper into engineering. Through my time at Conestoga, I was able to work at some awesome companies, and if you want you can check out some of my work.

Work

Formlabs

SLA Mechanical Engineering Intern Sept 2021 - Dec 2021

At Formlabs, I was part of the mechanical engineering team working on the next generation of Formlabs SLA 3D printers. While there, I headed the development of new resin design concepts, designing and building various electromechanical systems and creating the python programs to run them and MATLAB scripts to crunch the data.

I also developed parts for prototype printers such as electronics mounting brackets to create a system to easily mount all critical electronics and quickly move them from printer to printer. I also helped build and tram the first five prototypes of the next-generation Formlabs SLA 3D Printer.

Linamar

Design Engineer Co-op Sept 2018 - Dec 2018

At Linamar, I worked as a Design Engineer and designed jigs, fixtures, and pressing tools for a new production line manufacturing camshafts. I also created guarding for automated measuring machines to keep people safe and ensure that the measuring devices do not become contaminated. In addition to my design work on the camshaft line, I created FEA models to determine why a connecting rod splitting tool was not performing as expected. I used this data to design new tools that performed much better than the previous tool.

Tigercat

Equipment Designer - Drivetrain Group Co-op Jan 2018 - May 2018

At Tigercat, I worked in the drivetrain group, a small group of engineers working on creating drivetrain components that can withstand the demanding conditions of the forestry industry. While at Tigercat, I developed a prototype direct into mesh forced lubrication system for a hydraulic gearbox which required me to dive deep into the math, create calculators, conduct CFD and practical tests, and develop 3D printed prototypes of oil pathways. I also designed a bogie assembly stand capable of holding 4000kg, allowing quick changeovers from one bogie size to another with minimal change in tooling.

If you'd like to learn more about my work, you can download my resume. And if you'd like to contact me dont hesitate to use the contact form on this website!
